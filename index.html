Firoz Ahmed is an American mechanical engineer, R&D specialist, and entrepreneur based in Southlake, Texas. He currently serves as an R&D Engineer at Heat and Control, one of the world's leading manufacturers of industrial food processing equipment. His work spans fiber reinforced composites, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), finite element analysis (FEA), computer vision, machine learning, and embedded systems — a rare multidisciplinary profile operating at the intersection of mechanical engineering and artificial intelligence.

Ahmed is known for inventing a sound-based flow sensor using a MEMS microphone in a low-pressure regulator for PayGo Energy's Smart Gas Meter, and has filed multiple patents for innovations in sensing and industrial process control. He is currently developing reinforcement learning-trained autonomous drone software and will be competing in the AI Grand Prix 2026 autonomous drone racing competition.

Originally from Mangalore, Karnataka, India, Ahmed received his B.E. in Mechanical Engineering from Manipal Institute of Technology before immigrating to the United States in 2008. He earned his M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Texas A&M University-Kingsville in 2010, where his NASA-funded graduate research focused on the optimization of fiber-reinforced elastomer (FRE) composites for energy storage applications.[1]

Early life and education [edit]

Firoz Ahmed was born in Mangalore, Karnataka, India. He completed his Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Mechanical Engineering from Manipal Institute of Technology (MIT), Manipal, one of India's premier private engineering institutions.[1]

Ahmed immigrated to the United States in 2008 and enrolled at Texas A&M University-Kingsville, where he earned a Master of Science (M.S.) in Mechanical Engineering in August 2010. His thesis, Optimization of High Elongation Fiber-Reinforced Elastomer Composites, was supervised by Dr. Larry Peel, Ph.D., P.E. and was conducted as part of the NASA-funded Space Engineering Institute (SEI) program affiliated with the Texas A&M University System and mentored by NASA-Johnson Space Center.[1]

Ahmed's graduate research focused on optimizing fiber-reinforced elastomer (FRE) composite strips for use in a mechanical battery — a device intended to store energy through the mechanical stretching of elastomer-carbon fiber strips, analogous to a stretched rubber band, as an alternative to electrochemical batteries. His work involved fabricating FRE specimens using filament winding on an Entec filament winder and vacuum bag molding, testing them under cyclic tensile loads on a Universal Testing Machine, and optimizing fiber tow spacing (bandwidth) to maximize strain energy density and lifespan. He worked with IM7 carbon fiber and Freeman 1040 polyurethane elastomer, and determined that the 1.5 bandwidth configuration was optimal — achieving 50% strain without failure and storing the maximum energy for the mechanical battery application.[1]

Career [edit]

Texas A&M Kingsville (composites research)

As a graduate student and Space Engineering Institute member at Texas A&M Kingsville, Ahmed developed expertise in composite materials fabrication, including filament winding of IM7 carbon fiber tows at controlled angles (±45°, ±60°, ±75°) and bandwidths onto tapered steel mandrels, vacuum bag consolidation, hand layup of carbon cloth, and press-and-platen curing. He designed and fabricated custom test grips for the Universal Testing Machine, developed a heat lamp curing system to address humidity-related curing issues, and created a geometric Excel model for predicting FRE strip deformation. His experimental work included mechanical testing (stress-strain characterization, hysteresis analysis, stress relaxation), fiber volume fraction determination (resin burn-out and water immersion methods per ASTM 2584), and comparison with predicted values from Classical Lamination Theory software.[1]

Sun King / PayGo Energy

Ahmed served as Principal Engineer (Hardware) at Sun King (formerly PayGo Energy), a company focused on clean cooking technology and off-grid energy products for markets in Africa and Asia.[2] At PayGo Energy, he was directly involved in the development of the company's IoT Smart Meter — the Cylinder Smart Meter (CSM), a pay-as-you-go LPG metering device that was the first to achieve ATEX Zone II certification for LPG applications.[3]

A key contribution during his tenure was the invention of a sound-based flow sensor using a MEMS microphone integrated into a low-pressure regulator. This acoustic sensing approach enabled non-intrusive gas flow measurement within PayGo's Smart Gas Meter, leveraging the correlation between acoustic signatures and flow rates through the regulator — an innovation that avoided the cost and complexity of traditional mechanical flow sensors while maintaining measurement accuracy in a safety-critical LPG environment. Ahmed led the creation of a custom PCBA (printed circuit board assembly) for the sensor system based on a Nordic Semiconductor microcontroller, managing contract hardware and firmware engineers through the board design, layout, and bring-up process.[4]

His broader responsibilities included 3D CAD modeling, FEA, hardware product development, and leading certification testing as a liaison between partner manufacturers, testing companies, and internal engineering. He also developed AT command interfaces for system diagnostics and built the AFC (Acoustic Flow Control) proof-of-concept test rig software.[4] A colleague noted that Ahmed "brought a different engineering perspective that was helpful to all members of the team."[5]

Heat and Control

Since December 2023, Ahmed has served as R&D Engineer – Product Development at Heat and Control Inc., one of the world's leading manufacturers of industrial food processing, conveying, seasoning, packaging, and inspection equipment, with a global manufacturing footprint exceeding 101,000 m². The role spans the company's Texas and California operations in a hybrid capacity.[6]

At Heat and Control, Ahmed leads end-to-end development of aerodynamic, fluid, and electromechanical systems from proof-of-concept through production beta, integrating mechanical design, sensors, and control systems. His work includes:

  • Developed air–liquid separation systems from concept to manufacturing using CFD, empirical models, and field validation
  • Built Gocator 3D vision system for real-time mass flow control in conveying lines
  • Designed and validated prototypes across mechanical, fluid, and PID control subsystems
  • Managed global engineering teams (Europe, U.S., Australia) with 24/7 development cycles
  • Led R&D reviews with executive leadership, translating technical risk into business decisions
  • Supported manufacturing scale-up and customer deployments across global facilities
  • Filed multiple patents in sensing and industrial process control

Product development [edit]

Ahmed's product development portfolio spans multiple industries and includes both commercial and research hardware:[4]

  • Gocator 3D vision-based conveying system (Heat and Control) — vision system using Gocator 3D sensors to set and maintain consistent mass flow rate in product conveying systems, enabling real-time closed-loop flow control
  • Liquid-air separation system (Heat and Control) — developed from concept to manufacturing using CFD simulation, empirical models, and field validation
  • IoT Smart Meter (PayGo Energy) — the Cylinder Smart Meter for pay-as-you-go LPG metering, the first ATEX Zone II certified LPG smart meter; Ahmed invented the acoustic flow sensing method using a MEMS microphone in the low-pressure regulator and led creation of a custom PCBA on a Nordic microcontroller
  • Topology-optimized components — FEA-driven structural redesigns achieving 57% mass reduction (1.3 kg → 0.56 kg) while maintaining structural requirements
  • Embedded projects — rovers, PSOC-based PID controllers for rack and pinion motion, laser rotation measurement systems, RPi-based test fixtures with video capture, and VPython motion detection rendering

Ahmed has filed multiple patents for innovations in sensing technology, industrial process control, and related systems.[7]

Ventures and side projects [edit]

RaBurn Ai

Ahmed founded RaBurn Ai, a multilingual voice AI agent designed for clinic scheduling. The product was built in TypeScript using the OpenAI Realtime API with multilingual support and calendar integration, targeting healthcare clinics through warm family-connected leads.

Autonomous drone software

Ahmed is currently developing reinforcement learning-trained autonomous drone software in preparation for the upcoming AI Grand Prix 2026 competition, applying RL techniques to train drone agents for autonomous navigation, obstacle avoidance, and real-time decision-making — combining his embedded systems background with modern AI methods for physical robotics applications.

The Foundry and OpenClaw

The Foundry is a micro-SaaS generation pipeline structured as a Scout → Analyst → Builder workflow for autonomous identification, validation, and construction of small software products. OpenClaw / Jarvis is a multi-agent system featuring SQLite-backed persistent memory, ChromaDB RAG, Slack integration, health monitoring sub-agents, and automated daily/weekly digest workflows.
